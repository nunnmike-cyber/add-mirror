Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 March 2026

Important: ADHD Mirror is a self-reflection tool, not a clinical assessment. It does not provide a medical diagnosis. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for any concerns about your health.

1. Who We Are

ADHD Mirror is operated by Mike Nunn, trading as a sole trader based in the United Kingdom.

Website: adhdmirror.com
Contact: privacy@adhdmirror.com

2. What Information We Collect

2a. Information you provide

When you use the ADHD Mirror self-assessment tool, you submit responses to questions about your experiences and behaviours. This information is:

  • Processed entirely within your browser
  • Not stored on our servers
  • Not linked to your identity in any way

We do not ask for your name, email address, or any other identifying information to use the free assessment.

2b. Information collected automatically

We use Google Analytics (GA4) to understand how visitors use the site. Google Analytics may collect:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Your approximate location (country/region level only)
  • Device type, browser, and operating system
  • How you arrived at the site (e.g. from a search engine or social media)

This data is aggregated and anonymised. We cannot identify you from this data. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

2c. Paid report (if applicable)

If you purchase a paid report, we collect the information necessary to process your payment via Stripe. This may include your name, email address, and payment card details. Stripe processes this information securely in accordance with their own privacy policy. We do not store full payment card details on our systems.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and improve the ADHD Mirror assessment tool
  • Understand how the site is used so we can improve it
  • Process payments for paid reports
  • Comply with our legal obligations

We do not sell your data. We do not use your data for advertising.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (UK GDPR)

We process your data under the following legal bases:

  • Legitimate interests: understanding site usage through anonymised analytics to improve the service
  • Contract: processing payments where you have purchased a paid report
  • Legal obligation: complying with applicable laws and regulations

5. Cookies

ADHD Mirror uses the following cookies:

  • Google Analytics cookies (_ga, _gid, _gat): used to distinguish users and throttle request rate. These expire after 2 years, 24 hours, and 1 minute respectively.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect how the site functions.

6. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Google Analytics (Google LLC): website analytics. Google's privacy policy →
  • Stripe (Stripe, Inc.): payment processing for paid reports. Stripe's privacy policy →
  • Vercel (Vercel Inc.): website hosting. Vercel's privacy policy →

These services may transfer data outside the UK. Where this occurs, appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR requirements.

7. Data Retention

  • Assessment responses are not retained by us — they are processed locally in your browser
  • Google Analytics data is retained for 14 months, after which it is automatically deleted
  • Payment and transaction records are retained for 7 years in accordance with HMRC requirements

8. Your Rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Request deletion of your personal data
  • Object to or restrict our processing of your personal data
  • Data portability (where applicable)
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent)

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@adhdmirror.com. We will respond within one month.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe we have handled your data unlawfully.

9. Children

ADHD Mirror is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

11. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact us at privacy@adhdmirror.com.
